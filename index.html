ALL ABOUT THAT BASS

Bass is the lowest tuned instrument in the string family, and unlike the other stringed instruments, it is tuned in 4ths, not 5ths.

The role in the orchrestra

The bass plays a supporting role in the orchestra, adding depth and richness into the sound, while also provding rythmic function.

History

The bass is the youngest of the stringed instruments, and it is the least standardized. What we now recognize as the string bass today, started to only come around in the mid-late 1600s. Even still, you will see a lot of different tunings for the string bass then, and different varitions of string count. Bass then could be anywhere from 3-6 strings! Even still today, you could still see many players using a 4 or 5 string bass using either a french over-hand gripped bow or the german under-hand gripped bow.
